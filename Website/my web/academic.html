Book Review

Gettysburg is one of the most important battles in the Civil War, and in that, Pickett's charge was the most important part, through its Triumph and Tragedy. Piston William Garrett,the uthor of this book reveiw, says, "Reardon explores why Gettysburg came to be regarded as the turning point of the Civil War and Pickett's Charge the high tide of the Confederacy." This shows how important Reardon thinks Pickett's Charge is. The bravery of the soldiers in this charge, which were mostly under the command of George Pickett, was very high and to be able to fight with all your might for what you think is right, that, is a sign of courage. Piston continues, "Reardon ends her study with the t 913 reunion, by which time Pickett's Charge was firmly established in the national mind as a symbol for courage and sacrifice." This even futher describes the importance of Pickett's Charge from Reardons point of view, and how Picketts charge was not just a major force against the Union, it is now a symbol.
