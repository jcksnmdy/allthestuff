Pickett's Charge

Background Info

In May 1863, Robert E. Lee’s Army of Northern Virginia smashed the Army of the Potomac at Chancellorsville. They were filled with confidence and so went on to invade the North a second time, the first being the Battle of Antietam. By doing this Lee hoped to gain recognition of the Confederacy by Britain and France and strengthen the cause of northern “Copperheads” who favored peace. Abraham Lincoln placed Major General George Gordon Meade to succeed Joseph Hooker, after he was reluctant to fight Lee's army of 75,000. Meade immediately ordered the pursuit of Lee.
