Most of the troops charging up the hill were under George Pickett's command, that is why it is called "Picketts Charge and, "After a long Confederate artillery bombardment, the Rebel force moved through the open field and up the slight rise of Cemetery Ridge," says History.com. This shows how soon after the rebels pounded the Union, the Confederates charged. History continues by saying, "On July 4, Lee began to withdraw his forces to Virginia." Lee did this because of the staggering losses in the whole Battle of Gettysburg. He had encountered many losses and decided he would retreat. Reading this, I wonder if the date that Lee withdrew had any relationship with July fourth as we know it today, or if it was just a coincidence.
